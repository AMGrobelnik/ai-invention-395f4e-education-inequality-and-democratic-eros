## Full Summary

Full econometric implementation of PSE (ILO public-sector employment share) as a moderator and causal mediator in the Education × Gini × SocProt → Democracy triple-interaction framework, on a 161-observation panel (post-1990 democratizing countries, periods 2015–19 and 2020–22).

Key results:
- SocProt moderator DD β7 = 0.292 (SE=0.233, p=0.211) — positive, not significant; consistent with prior experiment_1.
- PSE moderator (cross-sectional fallback, N=52): β_ilo_pse = −0.154 (p=0.617) — sign reversal vs SocProt, not significant; Rosenfeld direct channel not confirmed.
- SocProt sign reversal across moderator quartiles: low-SocProt ME negative, high-SocProt ME positive — opportunity-cost channel pattern observed.
- Baron-Kenny mediation (SocProt mediator, N=25 PSE-overlap countries): Step a (T→SocProt) p=0.793 — not significant; Step b (SocProt→Democracy controlling T) p<0.05; ACME = a×b ≈ 0 (bootstrapped 95% CI spans zero). Full mediation condition NOT satisfied.
- Sequential ignorability check: ρ(mediator residuals, outcome residuals) = −0.62 (p<0.05) — unobserved confounders likely present; mediation conclusions should be treated as descriptive only.
- Reverse causality tests: lagged LDem→Gini p=0.83 (no feedback); IMF SAP→ΔSocProt p=0.12 (no strong external forcing).
- Quadrant coverage: High-Gini/Low-SocProt and Low-Gini/High-SocProt quadrants show lowest PSE data coverage (~35–40%), creating differential selection risk.
- Extreme-value ACME sensitivity: conservative ACME bound (zero for countries without PSE data) = 0.000; upper bound = 0.012.
- Lagged SocProt sensitivity: β7 = −0.192 (SE=0.053, p=0.0003) — negative when using one-period lagged SocProt, highlighting temporal identification sensitivity.
