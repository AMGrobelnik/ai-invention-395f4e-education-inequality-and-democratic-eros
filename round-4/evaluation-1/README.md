## Full Summary

Implemented and executed six pre-registered robustness checks for the Giesselmann-Schmidt-Catran (2022) double-demeaning DD triple-interaction estimator (Education × Gini × SocProt → Liberal Democracy) across two education-measure specifications.

1. Wild Cluster Bootstrap (CGM 2008, B=999, Rademacher weights): UNDP MYS specification (N=95, G=58) yields bootstrap p-value = 0.070 for β₇ = 0.292 (SE = 0.233); WB Tertiary specification (N=137, G=86) yields bootstrap p-value = 0.091 for β₇ = 13.128 (SE = 9.990). Both are marginal: significant at 10%, not 5%.

2. SWIID Gini Measurement Uncertainty (500 MC draws from N(gini_disp, gini_disp_se²)): Mean β₇ = 0.026 across draws, fraction of draws with β₇ > 0 = 62.8%. Modest positive bias but high variance across measurement error realizations.

3. Selection-into-Doubly-Observed Diagnostic: Countries with ≥2 ILO observations have systematically higher Gini dispersion (p = 0.079), flagging non-random selection into the doubly-observed subsample. Social protection coverage and mean years of schooling show no differential selection.

4. Minimum Detectable Effect Power Curves: At the realized cluster count G=58 (UNDP), MDE = 0.311 (≈ 2.0 standard deviations of Liberal Democracy) under baseline error variance — substantially above the point estimate of β₇, confirming the study is underpowered to detect the estimated effect reliably. MDE curves plotted for G=20–80 under three σ_ε scenarios.

5. ILO Directly-Reported Subgroup Analysis: iter-3 7-period panel has ilo_directly_reported_fraction = 0.0 (all SDG 1.3.1 data, no ILO directly-reported observations). Fell back to iter-1 2-period panel (N=161, all directly_reported_any=True). OECD-excluded sample (N=95) is identical to the ILO-restricted sample; β₇ = 0.292 unchanged.

6. Robustness Summary Table: JSON + LaTeX-ready TSV assembled across all checks. Key verdict: marginal bootstrap significance (p ≈ 0.07–0.09), SWIID sign-consistency 63%, non-random selection concern, chronic underpowering at G=58.
