## Full Summary

This experiment implements a corrected reanalysis of the State-Dependent Education Trap (SDET) hypothesis using the Giesselmann-Schmidt-Catran (2022) double-demeaning (DD) triple-interaction estimator. Six targeted corrections are applied to the iter_2 results:

1. **Education imputation audit**: Confirms that 100% of period-2 (2020-22) education values are forward-filled from a frozen 2017 anchor (70/161 obs = 43.5% of total sample imputed). The within-SD of 0.026 years is an artifact of comparing 2015-19 averages against a 2017 endpoint, not genuine educational change.

2. **Acemoglu null (corrected)**: Entity FE are inestimable with T=1 per country after 5-year lagging. The pooled OLS cross-sectional replication (β=0.043, p<0.001) is reported correctly as a cross-sectional result, not a panel FE null.

3. **Bonferroni correction (k=6 sub-indices)**: v2jucomp (judicial compliance) has raw p=0.024 but fails Bonferroni threshold (α/6=0.0083, corrected p=0.144). It is reclassified as an exploratory directional finding requiring pre-registered replication. No sub-index survives family-wise correction.

4. **WVS IIX coding resolution**: The dataset label 'higher IIX = more independent' is incorrect. The data.py (5-v) inversion means higher stored value = higher trust = more state-compliant orientation. Under corrected coding, public sector workers in the low-SocProt/high-Gini critical quadrant show higher IIX (0.831) than private sector (0.799) — consistent with SDET predictions.

5. **Oster bounds characterization**: β7* = -12.29 is 42.1× larger in magnitude than the DD estimate (0.292) and reverses sign. The robustness threshold δ < 1/42 ≈ 0.024. The estimate cannot withstand even minimal proportional selection on unobservables; R² barely changes from naive (0.9487) to DD (0.9474) model.

6. **Lagged subsample diagnosis**: Contemporaneous DD on the N=37 countries surviving the 5-year lag gives β7=+0.292 (positive), opposite to the lagged spec sign reversal. This confirms the sign reversal is caused by the lag itself (education frozen at 2017, period-2 lag recovers 2015-19 values), not sample composition.

Main DD result: β7=0.292 (SE=0.233, p=0.211) — not statistically significant. Naive baseline: β7≈0.000 (p=0.466), demonstrating substantial within/between-country bias in naive FE-product estimators.

Dataset: vdem_ilo_gini_edu_panel_complete (161 obs, 96 countries, 2 periods). Analysis sample: N=95 obs, 58 countries after OECD exclusion (GDP PPP < $15,000 at transition). Output includes per-example fitted values from both naive and DD estimators, plus complete metadata for all six correction results.
